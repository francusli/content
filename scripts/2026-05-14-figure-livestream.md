# Figure Livestream Script

## Draft
Figure has raised $2B to build general-purpose humanoid robots. This week, what was supposed to be an 8-hour livestreamed deployment quickly evolved into a 46 hour marathon with no end in sight. So far, over 58000 packages have been processed by robots that the internet has named Bob, Frank, Gary, and Rose.

This is incredible, but you can also feel the undercurrent of anxiety. Let's talk about it.

Warehouse automation is not new. We've had machines moving, scanning, and sorting packages for decades. Even humanoids in logistics isn't new. What feels different here is the autonomy, the "physical AI". These humanoids aren't just repeating fixed motions, they can pursue goals, adapt to changing environments, and recover from failure. 

And that's where hte tension comes from, not from existing capabilities but the implications.

The First-order effect is job displacement and economic uncertainty, but what comes after, I'm realizing, is is that humanity may slowly lose our monopoly on purposeful action itself.

This is what feels existentially destabilizing. So yes, we can question the capabiltiies and timelines, but also: "What are the cultural, moral, and institutional implications?" 

If we're barreling into a world where AI agents become increasingly capable of steering the future, then humanity needs to redefine the value of both labor and agency itself.
