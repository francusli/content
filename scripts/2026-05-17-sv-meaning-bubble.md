Over the past 5 years, thousands of people have hit retirement wealth, yet layoffs are in full swing while entry level jobs are dissapearing-- everywhere else people are having AI tools shoved down their throats, while engineers are realizing their life's skill is becoming less and less useful. This all feels like it's changing overnight.

- If you're in corporate, you feel like you may be climbing the wrong ladder. Is it too late to join OAI or Ant? Will another startup's stock 10x next? 
- Mid to late middle managers feel paralyzed. They have families and don't have any AI skills, but they see the writing on the wall, the layoffs are hollowing out their class.
- There is a deep malaise around work and its future. Will any of this matter when AI makes it obselete in a few years? 

Overall, across the city, there is a desperation around riding the wave of AI and vibecoding your path to economic enlightenment, before it's too late. While there is an ever-strong contingent that believe AI will ever be good enough, but they see their teams losing headcount and budgets to resources to AI teams. 

Everyone is trying to find meaning in a world that is changing so quickly. So what should we do?

The German philospher, Heidegger had an answer to this. Authenticity. When I was in college, NVIDIA and OAI were seen as 2nd tier companies that you worked at because you coudln't get a job at FAANG. It wasn't a status symbol, it wasn't the next hot thing. Anthorpic was founded in 2021, when Silcon Valley was obsessed with Web3 and the Metaverse.

Heidegger believes that the only way out is to stop measuring yourself against everyone else and ask: what do you actually want to do from here? What do you think?
